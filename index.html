About Donation

Learn more about how you can save lives through organ and blood donation.

Blood Donation

What is Blood Donation?
Blood donation is a voluntary act where healthy individuals donate blood to help patients in need. It is screened, stored, and used for medical treatments, surgeries, and emergencies.

Why is it Important?
Blood is essential for surgeries, cancer treatment, trauma care, and chronic illnesses.

Who can donate?
Generally, healthy individuals aged 18-65, meeting basic health criteria, can donate blood.

Organ & Tissue Donation

What is Organ and Tissue Donation?
Organ Donation is the gift of an organ to a person with end-stage organ disease who needs a transplant. The donated organ may be from a deceased or living donor.

What is Transplantation?
A surgical operation where a failing or damaged organ is removed and replaced with a functioning one.

Who can donate?
Any person above 18 years who voluntarily authorizes the removal of any of their organs or tissues.
